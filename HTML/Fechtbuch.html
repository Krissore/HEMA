What is Kunst des Fechtens?

"Kunst des Fechtens" means "Art of Fighting" in German. It refers to a collection of medieval martial techniques originating in the Holy Roman Empire during the 14th-17th centuries.

Historical background

This martial tradition was recorded in illustrated manuscripts known as Fechtbücher. It focused on practical combat with longswords, messers, daggers, and more.

Notable Masters

  • Johannes Liechtenauer - Codified the system and its cryptic verses.
  • Peter von Danzig - Expanded and interpreted Liechtenauer's work.
  • Joachim Meyer - Introduced a more sport-oriented and inclusive version.

Weapons Used

The system included:

  • Longsword (Zweihänder)
  • Messer (German arming sword)
  • Dagger and Ringen (grappling)
  • Polearms like the halberd

Techniques and Guards

Key terms include:

  • Zornhau – The wrath cut
  • Fühlen – Feeling through the blade
  • Vom Tag, Alber, Pflug – Guard positions

Modern Revival (HEMA)

Today, Kunst des Fechtens lives on through Historical European Martial Arts (HEMA). Practitioners worldwide study original manuals and revive the techniques in clubs, tournaments, and sparring.
